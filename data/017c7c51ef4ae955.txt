March 26, 2026
App Store Connect update
You can now
declare your app's regulated medical device status
and provide the necessary details in the App Information section for your app in App Store Connect. Apps that are distributed in the European Union (EU)/European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom (UK), or United States (U.S.) and meet either of the following criteria are required to indicate whether they’re regulated medical devices:
Have a primary or secondary category of Health & Fitness or Medical
Have indicated your app contains “frequent” in the Medical or Treatment Information field in the age rating questionnaire in App Store Connect
In order to comply with regulatory requirements in certain jurisdictions, you can now enter your compliance information in the Business section of App Store Connect. This is to align with requirements for
Directive on Administrative Cooperation-7th Amendment
,
Part XX of the Income Tax Act
,
Model Reporting Rules for Digital Platforms
, and
Sharing Economy Reporting Regime
.
Minor editorial fixes were also made.

You can now choose to apply
Billing Grace Period
only to existing paid renewals or continue applying it to all subscription renewals (existing paid renewals and free offers transitioning to paid renewals). You can also choose a duration of 3, 16, or 28 days instead of having the duration predefined based on your subscription length. When you enable Billing Grace Period in App Store Connect, Apple attempts to address any subscriber billing issues and recover the subscription while subscribers retain access to subscription benefits. If the subscription is recovered within this period, there’s no interruption to the days of paid service or to your revenue.

As of January 1, 2023, the euro (EUR) is the official currency of Croatia, replacing the Croatian kuna (HRK). Price tiers in App Store Connect have be converted to euros using the statutory fixed conversion rate of 7.53450 HRK = 1.00000 EUR established by the Council of the European Union on July 12, 2022.
